Sjenica region was inhabited in prehistoric times. The remains of a prehistoric fort were found on the perimeter of Sjenica field, on the Zarudina hill, near Sjenica, and at several sites near Sjenica necropolises were discovered and objects originating from prehistoric period were found. In this area as well as beyond, Dardanians lived.
Sjenica was first mentioned in 1253. as a place on the Dubrovnik road, where the merchants of Dubrovnik approached and paid customs. The Turks, on a lofty part, erected a fort (City) which was later destroyed. Near the fort is a street with shops and log houses and planks.












The most significant historical figure of the Sjenica
Hasan Zvizdic (1892 – 1980)
Better known as the man who rescued Bosniaks in Sandzak in World War II.
Hasan was born in 1892 in Sjenica and died in 1980.
Hasan was a respected and respected figure in Turkey as well.
