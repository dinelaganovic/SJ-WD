General data

By area (1,059 km square) Sjenica is one of the largest municipalities in Serbia.
According to 2011 Census data, 26,392 people live in Sjenica. Of that number, 12,958 (49.10%) are female and 13,434 (50.90%) are male.
Sjenica is a multinational community and is inhabited by Bosniaks, Serbs, Albanians, Montenegrins, Turks, Roma and others.
In terms of ethnicity, there are 19,498 (73.88%) Bosniaks, 5,264 Serbs (19.95%), and 1,234 Muslims. (4.68%), Roma 93 (0.35%), Albanians 29 (0.11%) and members of other nationalities 274 (1.04%).
According to the level of development of local self-government units for 2014, the municipality of Sjenica is in the fourth group consisting of 44 markedly underdeveloped local self-government units whose development rate is below 60% of the national average.

Geographical position

Sjenica is a small town and municipality of the same name, located in the southwestern part of Serbia, on the vast Sjenica-Pester plateau and geographically located in the central part of Sandzak. The town extends to the right of the Uvac river and is located along the Grabovica river, in the vast Sjenica basin, at an altitude of 1,000-1,030 m. By area (1,059 km square) Sjenica is one of the largest municipalities in Serbia. Sjenica is located in the mountainous surroundings of Golija (1.833 m), Jadovnik (1.733 m), Ozren (1.693 m), Zlatar (1.625 m), Giljeva (1.617 m), Zilindar (1.616 m), Javor (1.519 m), Ninaje ( 1,362 m), Jaruta (1,428 m) and other mountain ranges, on one side, and vast basins of Sjenica, Pester , Kostans and other fields. It borders with the municipalities of Bijelo Polje (Montenegro), Prijepolje, Tutin, Novi Pazar, Ivanjica and Nova Varos. The area of ​​the Sjenica-Pester plateau is abundant in extremely high natural resources, suitable especially for the development of agriculture and animal husbandry, as well as significant mineral resources (large deposits of brown lignite coal). In addition to its other specific features, Sjenica is also known in Europe as one of the coldest populated places. Winters in Sjenica are long and cold and low temperatures can sometimes last for days (in January 2005., in Sjenica, the lowest temperature in Serbia of - 42 degrees Celsius was also recorded). Officially, the lowest measured temperature in Sjenica - 39 degrees Celsius was recorded on January 26, 2006., in K.B.. In January 26, 1954., it measured -38.4 degrees Celsius. Extraordinary natural advantages and a developed tradition of winter sports, the area of ​​Sjenica municipality makes one of the greatest potentials for the development of winter sports, as well as summer recreational and excursion tourism, in the Republic of Serbia and beyond. In this sense, Sjenica has a number of ski runs, as well as the National Biathlon Center "Zari". Other potentials include the nature reserves of Uvac, Gutavica and Paljevina, Sjenica Lake, the large Pesters field, Ear Cave System, Tubic Cave, meanders of the Vape river, as well as numerous archeological sites and sites.
